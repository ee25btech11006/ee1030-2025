\textbf{Question}:\\
Using elementary transformations, find the inverse of the following matrix. 
\begin{align*}
    \myvec{2&&3\\-4&&-6}
\end{align*}

\solution \\
The determinant is:
\begin{align}
\det \myvec{2 & 3 \\ -4 & -6} = (2)(-6) - (3)(-4) = -12 + 12 = 0
\end{align}
Since the determinant is zero, the matrix is singular and hence \textbf{not invertible}.
